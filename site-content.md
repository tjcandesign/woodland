## Page: The Closing Process (/the-closing-process)

### Section: Title Search
**Subhead:** "Ensuring Your Property Rights"

"We order and rigorously review the title history—and resolve any defects prior to closing; so you can enjoy your property free of any encumbrances."

They arrange property surveys to clarify exact boundaries and confirm no other party holds claims to the property.

### Section: Title Insurance
**Subhead:** "Guaranteeing Future Property Claims"

"Title insurance protects both you and your lender against any obstacles to clear and confident ownership."

Lenders require a one-time policy; homeowners may purchase additional coverage against future claims.

### Section: Escrow
**Subhead:** "Holding The Settlement Funds in Trust & Disbursement"

"Once you have entered a contract for the purchase of your new home, you will be required to produce earnest money."

Funds remain in trust until closing, when disbursements address liens, taxes, utilities, recording fees, and the home price according to contract terms. Closing costs are presented three days prior to settlement.

## Page: COVID-19 Practices & Protocols (/covid-19-practices-protocols)

### For Sellers

**E-Close:**
Sellers in Washington, DC, Virginia, and Maryland can close remotely using Notarize.com with a $99.00 fee. Strongly encouraged.

**In-Office:**
Safety measures: mask-wearing, six-foot distancing, surface disinfection before and after closings, hand-washing/sanitizer use, new pens provided. Documents delivered as searchable PDFs through secure portal.

### For Buyers / Refinance Borrowers

**In-Office:**
Most team members work remotely. Office staff follow CDC best practices: masks at all times, six-foot distancing, surface wiping before/after closing, hand-washing/sanitizer, new pens provided (take home).

**From Home:**
Mobile notary services available ($160.00 fee) since lenders don't permit e-notarization.

**Cash Buyers, E-Close:**
Cash purchasers may e-close in all three jurisdictions without lender documents.

### For Real Estate Agents & Lenders
Agents and loan officers welcome at settlements with client preference verified. E-closing documents reviewed via conference call; clients then notarize through Notarize.com. Office visitors should follow CDC preventative actions.

### EMD & Cash to Close
Wire transfers are strongly encouraged.

## Sanity CMS Content Model Notes

Based on this content, the recommended Sanity schema structure:

### Document Types
1. **page** — Generic page (Home, Security, COVID, Code of Conduct)
   - title, slug, hero (heading + body + image), sections[]
2. **service** — Individual service items
   - title, description, audience (buyers/sellers vs professionals)
3. **closingStep** — Steps in the closing process
   - title, subhead, body, order
4. **utilityListing** — Utility provider entries
   - provider name, region (DC/MD/VA), county/city, phone, email, url
5. **contactInfo** — Singleton for office info
   - address, phone, fax, email
6. **siteSettings** — Singleton for global config
   - site title, copyright, nav links, footer links, underwriters, external CTAs

### Key Considerations
- Utilities page has the most structured data — ideal for Sanity's reference/array patterns
- COVID page may become obsolete — consider making it toggleable via siteSettings
- Services and Closing Process are good candidates for reorderable arrays
- Contact info should be a singleton referenced across all pages (footer)
